Limitations A tool that hides its weaknesses is more dangerous than one that names them.

Cross-sectional data
All three component functions (ASHE earnings, EQ-5D health, ONS well-being) are derived from cross-sectional surveys: different people at different ages, not the same cohort tracked over time. The age profiles capture population-level patterns, not individual trajectories. Cohort effects in earnings and well-being may distort the profiles.
S(t) enters only in the lifetime integral
M(t) is defined without survival. S(t) multiplies M(t) only in the lifetime integral and in Chart 5 ("Survival-Weighted Value"). The two are computed separately in the code. There is no double-counting.
Normative choices are explicit, not empirical
The Cobb-Douglas penalty (η) and the leisure dividend (δ) are modelling choices, not empirical results. Without them, the retirement-age curve is monotone and the interior optimum disappears. Set η = 0 or δ = 0 to remove either mechanism. This is a conditional calculator, not an oracle.
Questionnaire is preference elicitation, not calibration
The ten-question mapping to γ, η, δ, ρ is theoretically motivated but empirically unvalidated. There is no evidence yet that the derived parameters predict observed saving, consumption, or retirement timing. The "Direct Sliders" mode bypasses the questionnaire entirely.
Normalisation asserts commensurability
Mapping E, F, U to [0,1] loses the fact that earnings vary by ~8× across the lifespan while EQ-5D varies by only ~0.25. Weighting raw dimensions would require an explicit exchange rate between £/week, QALY-utilities, and ONS happiness points. The normalisation is a simplifying assumption, not a claim of demonstrated equivalence.
No uncertainty quantification
The model produces point estimates from median values at sparse age points. There are no confidence intervals, bootstrap, or Monte Carlo propagation. All outputs should be treated as indicative explorations of structural trade-offs, not as financial recommendations. This is the most important gap.
Data vintage
PLSA targets: June 2025 update (£13,400 / £31,700 / £43,900 one-person). State Pension: £241.30/week (£12,548/year) from April 2026. ASHE 2024 age-band anchors retained (2025 release shifts E(t) up ~5% uniformly without altering shape). EQ-5D anchors from HSE 2014 remain the most recent publicly available sex-averaged values.
